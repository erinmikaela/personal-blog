Atomic Habits

by: James Clear

I recently finished reading Atomic Habits by James Clear, and I can honestly say it's one of the most practical and motivating books I've come across in a long time. I picked it up because I wanted to make some positive changes in my life but often found myself struggling to stick with new habits. Clear's method struck a chord with me right away since it was refreshingly simple and practical.

Since reading Atomic Habits, I've been experimenting with a few new habits, using the Four Laws to guide me. For example, I've started a habit of journaling every night. I made it obvious by keeping my journal next to my bed, I made it easy by setting a goal to write just one sentence if I'm tired, and I made it satisfying by marking each day off in a calendar when I complete it. After a few weeks, it feels more natural, and I'm already noticing the benefits.

Anyone who is serious about creating long-lasting changes in their lives, regardless of how big or little should read Atomic Habits, in my opinion. The lessons I've learned from this book are things I can see myself using for years to come.

The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes

by: Arthur Conan Doyle

The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes was one of my favorite reads of all time. This book is the first collection of short stories Arthur Conan Doyle wrote about Sherlock Holmes. Each work narrates the various cases that the detective works on, accompanied by his former roommate and colleague Dr. John Watson, who narrates the tales.

While I enjoyed all twelve stories, a few really stood out for me. A Scandal in Bohemia, the first story, introduces Irene Adler, one of the few people who truly challenges Holmes. I loved the twist and the respect Holmes has for her by the end. The Red-Headed League was another favorite. It's such a bizarre case on the surface, but Holmes' solution is ingenious and unexpected. And then there's The Adventure of the Speckled Band, it was definitely eerie and suspenseful that I couldn't put it down until I found out the ending. Each story had something unique to offer, but these three kept me hooked the most.

The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes is a classic for a reason. The storytelling, the intriguing mysteries, and the dynamic between Holmes and Watson make it a truly enjoyable read. It's the kind of book that kept me guessing and trying to solve the cases alongside Holmes, even though I rarely figured it out before he did. If you're a fan of mysteries or just want to experience the iconic detective in action, I highly recommend this book. There's a reason Sherlock Holmes series is still pretty popular in the genre, as his adventures are as captivating now as they were when they were first published.
